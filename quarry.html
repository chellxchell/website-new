Bio Resumé

A Conversation Data Interface

I worked as a front-end developer on Quarry, a conversational data access system developed for a computer science systems class. Quarry provides simple answers for individuals who lack the technical proficiency to query databases. Due to the incredibly large volume of text and data that goes into these databases, it becomes nearly impossible for people with limited technical experience to access the data.

Intuitive Branding

Logo

When designing a logo, I wanted to create something fun but still professional. I decided to make the word "Quarry" an illustration, with the "Q" resembling a search icon. I reused the design of this "Q" in the search bar of the interface itself, giving the design a sense of consistency.

Colors

After deciding on a logo, I wanted to pick a minimalistic color scheme that would make the interface easy to use and navigate. I decided on a light blue, which would make Quarry visually appealing yet not overwhelming to users.

Wireframing

I began working on the interface by creating a mockup in Sketch. I wanted the path for the user to be simple - type the desired dataset, enter a query, and view the results.

Digital Prototype

After mocking up the interface, I started to bring it to life in React. I made two different routes - one leading to the home page of the application, and another leading to the information from the query.

Moving Forward

My team plans to complete a demo of this project at the end of the quarter using a data set of law cases we obtained from a Northwestern professor. We are currently in the process of integrating the front-end and back-end components to bring the project together.
